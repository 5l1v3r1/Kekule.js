Conformation of cyclohexane

In 1890, Hermann Sachse, a 28-year-old assistant in Berlin, published instructions for folding a piece of paper to represent two forms of cyclohexane () he called symmetrical and unsymmetrical (what we would now call chair and boat).

To embed a chemical structure in HTML, you have to prepare chemical data first. Now the simplest way is to insert chemical format file data into HTML <script> tag, e.g.:

<script name="ethane" id="ethane" type="chemical/x-mdl-molfile">

Then you can refer to that resource by ID in your HTML code. For example, to display its 2D structure, just use the following tag:

<div id="div1" data-kekule-role="viewer2D" data-chem-obj="url(#ethane)"></div>

Result:

And you can also set special display params inside the tag by data-params attribute:

<div data-kekule-role="viewer2D" data-chem-obj="url(#ethane)" data-params='{"drawOptions": {"zoom": 2, "fontSize": 30, "fontFamily": "Times New Roman"}}'></div>

Similar to draw a 3D structure:

<div class="Div3D" data-kekule-role="viewer3D" data-chem-obj="url(#CycloHex-Chair)" data-params='{"drawOptions": {"moleculeDisplayType": 4}}'> </div>
